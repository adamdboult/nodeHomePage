0.1 Properties of determinants

0.1.1 Identity

det I = 1

0.1.2 Multiplication

det(AB)=det Adet B

0.1.3 Inverse

$\det (M^{-1})=\dfrac{1}{\det M}$

We know this because:

det(MM−1)=det I = 1

det Mdet M−1 = 1

$\det (M^{-1})=\dfrac{1}{\det M}$

0.1.4 Complex cojugate

$\det (M^*)=\overline {\det M}$

0.1.5 Transpose

det(MT)=det M

0.1.6 Addition

det(A + B)=det A + det B

0.1.7 Scalar multiplication

det cM = cndet M

0.1.8 Determinants and eigenvalues

The determinant is equal to the product of the eigenvalues.
