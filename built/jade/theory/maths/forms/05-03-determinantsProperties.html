Properties of determinants

Identity

det I = 1

Multiplication

det(AB)=det Adet B

Inverse

$\det (M^{-1})=\dfrac{1}{\det M}$

We know this because:

det(MM−1)=det I = 1

det Mdet M−1 = 1

$\det (M^{-1})=\dfrac{1}{\det M}$

Complex cojugate

$\det (M^*)=\overline {\det M}$

Transpose

det(MT)=det M

Addition

det(A + B)=det A + det B

Scalar multiplication

det cM = cndet M

Determinants and eigenvalues

The determinant is equal to the product of the eigenvalues.
