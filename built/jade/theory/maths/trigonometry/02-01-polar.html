Polar co-ordinates

All complex numbers can be shown in polar form

Consider a complex number

z = a + bi

We can write this as:

z = rcos(θ)+irsin(θ)

Polar forms are not unique

Because the functions loop:

aeiθ = a(cos(θ)+isin(θ))

aeiθ = a(cos(θ + nτ)+isin(θ + nτ))

aeiθ = aeiθ + nτ

Additionally:

aeiθ = a(cos(θ)+isin(θ))

aeiθ = a(cos(θ)+isin(θ))

aeiθ = −a(cos(θ)−isin(θ))

$ae^{i\theta }=-a(\cos(\theta +\frac{\pi }{2})+i\sin(\theta +\frac{\pi }{2}))$

Real and imaginary parts of a complex number in polar form

We can extract the real and imaginary parts of this number.

Re(z):=rcos(θ)

Im(z):=rsin(θ)

Alternatively:

$Re(z)=r\frac{e^{i\theta }+e^{-i\theta }}{2}$

$Im(z)=r\frac{e^{i\theta }-e^{-i\theta }}{2i}$
