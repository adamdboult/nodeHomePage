0.1 The exterior (wedge) product

The exterior product of two vectors is:

u ∧ v

0.2 The exterior product is anticommutative

This is anticommutative (alternating).

u ∧ v = −v ∧ u

This implies that:

u ∧ u = 0

0.3 The exterior product is distributive

(a + b)∧(c + d)=(a ∧ c)+(a ∧ d)+(b ∧ c)+(b ∧ d)

0.4 Expanding the exterior product of two vectors

Consider 2-dimenional vector space with the following vectors:

u = ae1 + be2

v = ce1 + de2

The exterior product is:

u ∧ v = (ae1 + be2)∧(ce1 + de2)

u ∧ v = (ae1 ∧ ce1)+(ae1 ∧ de2)+(be2 ∧ ce1)+(be2 ∧ de2)

u ∧ v = ac(e1 ∧ e1)+ad(e1 ∧ e2)+bc(e2 ∧ e1)+bd(e2 ∧ e2)

u ∧ v = ad(e1 ∧ e2)−bc(e1 ∧ e2)

u ∧ v = (ad − bc)(e1 ∧ e2)
